↓Digital Object 04 - Geo-Spatial Structures
Noise levels in Downtown Manhattan, sourced from 519,200 noise complaints received by NYC311 from 2010 to 2025. The average number of complaints during each hour is normalized with a recency weight and a time weight to create the final visualization. The recency weight places more emphasis on recent complaints, while the time weight adjusts for natural fluctuations in complaint volume across the day. Since complaint activity peaks during nighttime hours—especially when people are trying to sleep—the time weight helps correct for this bias, ensuring that higher complaint counts at night don’t automatically imply louder environments. This creates a more balanced, time-aware view of urban noise patterns.
